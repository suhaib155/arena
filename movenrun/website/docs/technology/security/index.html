Security

MovenRun protects two things at once: the users who play, and the protocol that holds their ownership. Security is designed in at every layer.

Movement integrity

The first line of defense is proving movement is real. Plausibility checks, deterministic proofs, and deduplication keep spoofed or replayed activity out of the economy, and the verification layer is designed to keep hardening as the network grows. See Movement verification.

Account and asset safety

  • User-controlled keys. Deeds and $MOVE are held under keys the user controls, even when the wallet is managed invisibly.
  • Authenticated actions. Onchain operations are authorized by the user's wallet, so no one can act on their assets for them.
  • Self-custody available. Players can export or connect their own wallet and take full control at any time.

Protocol security

  • Role-based permissions. Privileged contract actions are gated by explicit, minimal roles — no single omnipotent key.
  • Timelocked governance. Sensitive changes pass through a delay, so the community can see and respond before execution.
  • Standards-based contracts. The protocol builds on well-established, audited onchain patterns rather than reinventing primitives.
  • Defense in depth. Verification, authentication, roles, and timelocks each limit what a single failure can do.

Anti-abuse

Because the economy rewards activity, it's designed to resist gaming: verification blocks fake movement, rate limits and per-period caps blunt farming, and challenge mechanics keep territory earned rather than hoarded. Integrity is treated as a core feature, not an afterthought — it's what makes ownership worth having.
