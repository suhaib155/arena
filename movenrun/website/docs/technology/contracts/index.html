Protocol & contracts

MovenRun's onchain protocol is a set of coordinated contracts on Base that turn verified movement into ownership, economy, and governance.

The protocol at a glance

The protocol is the system of record for everything that matters: who owns which zones, how much $MOVE exists and moves, how territory is contested, how seasons resolve, and how the community governs. Verified movement, attested by the oracle, is the input that drives it.

The contracts

The MovenRun protocol contracts and their roles
ContractRole
$MOVE tokenThe network's asset. Minted by verified movement within a capped schedule; used and burned across the game.
Zone DeedsThe ownership primitive — one onchain deed per map zone, held under the owner's keys, with provenance, yield, and defense.
ChallengeThe contest system: challengers stake to compete for a held zone; movement is weighed and the deed holds or changes hands.
Season controllerSchedules seasons and city competition, and coordinates periodic economic events.
VaultStaking and the community treasury — the economic reserve the network draws on.
GovernanceOnchain proposals and voting, secured by a timelock, controlling protocol parameters and the treasury.
Movement oracleAttests verified proofs of movement so the contracts above can act on real-world activity.

Security-first design

  • Trust-minimized. Contracts act on signed proofs of movement, never on unverified client input.
  • Role-scoped. Privileged actions are gated by explicit roles, not a single all-powerful key.
  • Timelocked. Sensitive changes pass through a delay, giving the community time to see and react.
  • Standards-based. The protocol builds on well-established onchain patterns for tokens, ownership, and governance.

Details of the asset design are in the $MOVE economy; ownership mechanics in Zone Deeds; community control in Governance.
