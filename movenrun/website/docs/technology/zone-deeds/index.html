Zone Deeds

Zone Deeds are MovenRun's ownership primitive — the onchain assets that make territory genuinely yours.

One deed, one zone

Each captured zone maps to a single Zone Deed: a unique onchain asset identified by that zone's place on the global grid. A deed is held under the owner's keys and behaves like real property inside the game — you can hold it, strengthen it, defend it, and transfer it, and its history is recorded onchain.

What a deed carries

  • Ownership & provenance. Who holds it now, and the history of how it changed hands.
  • Defense. A strength that rises with active holding and fortification and is tested by challenges.
  • Yield & loyalty. Active holders accrue in-game yield, with loyalty that rewards long-term stewardship.
  • Composability. Deeds group into districts and city power, and interoperate with the rest of the protocol.

Holding, defending, transferring

Deeds are living assets. A held zone can be challenged through the challenge system: a challenger stakes to compete, movement is weighed, and the deed either holds or transfers based on the outcome — settled onchain, without any middleman deciding. Owners can also transfer deeds directly. Territory that survives a season of contests is territory that means something.

Owned by you, managed for you

Deeds are real onchain assets, but claiming and holding them requires nothing crypto from the player — the wallet, the gas, and the transactions are handled invisibly (see Onchain, made invisible). Crypto-native owners can self-custody and interact with their deeds directly; everyone else simply owns.
