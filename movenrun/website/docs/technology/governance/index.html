Governance & the DAO

MovenRun is built to be owned by the people who move on it. Governance is onchain, timelock-secured, and progressively decentralized.

How governance works

  1. Propose. Stakeholders put forward changes — to parameters, the treasury, or the protocol itself.
  2. Vote. Voting power is based on a governance snapshot, so influence can't be gamed by shuffling tokens.
  3. Timelock. Passed proposals wait through a delay, giving the community time to review before anything takes effect.
  4. Execute. After the delay, the change executes onchain, transparently.

The community treasury

The network's vault holds a community treasury that governance directs — funding growth, rewards, partnerships, and the ongoing development of the network. Because it's onchain and timelock-secured, allocation is transparent and accountable to the people who hold the network.

Progressive decentralization

Decentralization done responsibly is a journey, not a switch. MovenRun begins with active stewardship — so the network can move fast and stay safe while it's young — and deliberately hands more control to the community as the system proves itself. The destination is a network whose parameters, treasury, and direction are set by the players who move on it.

Governance principles

  • Onchain and transparent — proposals, votes, and execution are all visible.
  • Snapshot-based voting — resistant to manipulation.
  • Timelocked execution — no surprise changes; the community always has time to react.
  • Progressive — control moves to the community as the network matures.
