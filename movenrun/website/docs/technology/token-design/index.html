Token, economics & governance

MovenRun's economic design is a set of guardrails plus one legacy experiment — not an offering. This page uses the title “token and economic design” rather than “how to earn” because there is nothing to earn.

Conditional — everything token-related is subject to redesign, independent audit, legal review, and product-readiness gates.

In-app progress vs. transferable assets

The distinction every page in these docs maintains
In-app progressTransferable assets
ExamplesXP, streaks, movement points (“Locked MOVE” label)Tokens, NFTs
Where it livesOn your device, in the gameWould live on-chain
Exists today?YesNo (testnet experiments only, no consumer access)
Redeemable / tradable?No — explicitly “not a payout”Nothing exists to trade; no marketplace is promised

“Locked MOVE” in the current app is a display preview derived from XP — no balance is stored and nothing accrues. The historical V1 design also imagined a capped “Liquid MOVE” for committed players, strictly behind density and verification gates; that concept remains exactly that — a gated historical design, not a feature.

Legacy experimental V1 parameters Historical

The V1 testnet experiment shipped with a full tokenomics design: a 1-billion supply cap, per-kilometer oracle-gated emission with periodic halvings and daily caps, an auto-valve that reduces emission when burn activity is low, burn sinks on game actions, a 2% zone tax accruing to deed holders with loyalty multipliers, and 90-day seasons ending in a supply burn. Internal documents also sketched target supply allocations, including a public-sale bucket, and a future staking-earned governance token.

Treat all of it as legacy experimental design. These parameters exist to make the testnet experiment concrete. They are not commitments, not current offerings, and several interact with V1 defects that the V2 redesign fixes (the season “burn” among them). Any production economy would be redesigned from guardrails up, audited independently, and legally reviewed — and no allocation, sale, price, or yield is promised by anything in this section. MovenRun currently promises no liquid reward economy at all.

The guardrails that outrank the parameters

  • No liquid rewards before reliable verification, real density, and genuine demand (the standing project rule).
  • The game must remain complete and enjoyable with zero economy.
  • Caps and sinks before emissions; conservative defaults; no retroactive enrichment.
  • No sale CTAs, no exchange links, no price discussion — in the product or in these docs.

Governance status

  • Today: there is no operational governance and no decentralization claim. Product decisions are made by the team.
  • Experimental testnet — V1 includes a custom three-tier DAO contract with documented weaknesses (live-balance voting; a vault role that was never wired).
  • Under review — the V2 proposal replaces it with standard snapshot governance (OpenZeppelin Governor, quorum fraction, timelock).
  • Long-term: community participation in territory-level decisions is a design goal, adopted only when real and only after the same gates as everything else.
