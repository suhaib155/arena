Web3 overview

Web3 is MovenRun's record layer, not its business model — optional by architecture, not by marketing.

Why optional

The core game — sessions, territory, clubs, progression — is complete without any chain. That is a design invariant: if removing Web3 would break the product, the product is built wrong. A wallet is never required to move, capture, join clubs, or progress, and no purchase is required to participate.

What Web3 may add

One narrow thing, for committed players who want it: transparent, portable territory records. A Zone Deed would let a player hold their claim to a zone as an on-chain record with public provenance — useful if you've spent a year making a district yours and want that history to be durable and verifiable. Base is the intended chain: low-cost, EVM-compatible, consumer-oriented.

What stays off-chain

  • All movement data — routes never go on-chain; at most, a verification proof (a hash and signature) would.
  • Everyday gameplay — capture, defense, streaks, clubs, XP.
  • Identity — no wallet-as-login for the core game.

No earning promise

MovenRun is not move-to-earn and makes no earning claim. No token has a price, a sale, or a yield here. In-app progress is not redeemable. If a token economy is ever proposed for production, it arrives through audits, legal review, and the release gates — and the docs will say so plainly at that time.
