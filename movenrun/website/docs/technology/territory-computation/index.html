Territory computation

How a wandering GPS route becomes crisp, ownable territory: one global hex grid, and a clean way to turn movement into map.

A single grid for the whole world

MovenRun models the planet with H3, an open hexagonal grid system that tiles the entire Earth into cells. Every location belongs to exactly one cell with a stable identifier, which gives the game a shared, precise definition of "a zone" — the same for every player, everywhere. That shared grid is what makes territory ownership consistent and composable.

Why hexagons

  • Even neighbors. Every hexagon borders six others at equal distance, so adjacency — central to holding, spreading, and defending territory — behaves cleanly.
  • Natural fit to movement. Routes curve; hexagons approximate the reach around a path far better than squares.
  • Clean aggregation. Cells roll up into larger cells, which is how zones combine into districts and cities.

From route to territory

  1. Each point on a verified route is resolved to its containing hex cell.
  2. Consecutive duplicates collapse into the ordered set of zones the route crossed.
  3. That set drives onchain capture — the zones become deeds you own.

Zones, districts, cities

Because H3 cells nest, MovenRun composes territory at multiple scales: individual zones you capture, districts that group them into neighborhoods with their own mastery, and cities where clubs contest control. One grid underpins the entire game, from a single street to a citywide season.
