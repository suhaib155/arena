Movement sessions & routines

The session flow is the heart of the current beta: start, move, finish, review — then come back tomorrow.

Implemented in the current mobile build, on-device.

Starting a session

From the Today screen, Start Move opens the session flow: a location-permission gate (first time), a mode context (walk, run, or cycle), then a live session screen. The app uses foreground GPS only — tracking runs while the session screen is active, and there are no background location services, geofencing, or push notifications.

During the session

  • The route draws live as you move.
  • Distance, elapsed time, and pace stay readable at a glance — deliberately simple so attention stays on your surroundings, not the screen.
  • Pause and resume are always available.

Finishing and the route summary

Finishing produces a summary: distance, duration, pace, the route shape, XP gained, and the territory the route touched, leading into the capture moment. Sessions award progress once per local day per goal-type to keep the loop honest (an anti-farming rule inherited from the earliest builds).

Routines: recaps, objectives, and saved progress

  • Weekly recap — a calm 7-day reflection of movement and territory progress, derived on demand from on-device state.
  • Season objectives — clear weekly gameplay goals that sit between alerts and the recap.
  • Route history & review — recent sessions with privacy-safe summaries (see Privacy).
  • Onboarding questline — a guided first week: first route, first capture, first defend, first club, first passport.

Verification previews

Each summary includes a Route Trust preview — a deterministic, locally computed 0–100 score that previews the kind of GPS-quality verification a future backend will perform. A Route Review History and a Route Signal Passport track the trend, and a shareable Route Proof card celebrates results using summary stats only. These are Local preview systems: no backend is consulted and no reward depends on them.

Safety and attention principles

  • Live UI is glanceable by design; nothing requires interaction mid-movement.
  • No mechanic rewards speed over safety — there is no incentive to sprint through an intersection to hold a zone.
  • Alerts are in-app only; the product never buzzes you into the street.

Current limitations

  • Foreground tracking only: switching away for long periods can interrupt a session; battery use is that of an active GPS app.
  • GPS accuracy varies in dense urban canyons and indoors; the trust preview surfaces this rather than hiding it.
  • Sessions live on your device; there is no account system or cross-device sync yet.
