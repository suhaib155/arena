Territory & ownership

The world is a hexagonal map, and every hex is a piece of property waiting to be claimed. Move to capture it; hold it to own it; fortify it to keep it.

What a zone is

A zone is one hexagonal cell of the real world — the atom of MovenRun territory. Capture one and it becomes a Zone Deed, an onchain asset you hold under your own keys. Zones are consistent for everyone: two players standing on the same corner are in the same zone, everywhere on Earth. The grid comes from the open H3 system — explained simply in Territory computation.

Capturing and holding

Finishing a session claims the zones your route crossed. Holdings persist onchain and appear across your map, your profile, and each zone's detail view. The more you return, the stronger your claim.

The life of a zone

  • Captured → Held — the zone joins your portfolio as a deed.
  • Held → Contested — neglect leaves a zone open to challenge from other players.
  • Fortified — returning and fortifying raise a zone's defensive strength.
  • Defended — strong zones withstand challenges; weak ones can change hands.

Districts and cities

Zones group into named districts, and districts into cities. Holding a critical mass of a district earns mastery and standing; controlling districts is how clubs and crews contest a city. Territory is a ladder — from a single street you own, to a district you dominate, to a city your club is fighting for. See Clubs, cities & competition.

Contesting territory

Zones aren't static. A held zone can be challenged through the onchain contest system: challengers stake to compete, both sides' movement is weighed, and the deed changes hands or holds based on the outcome. Defense, fortification, and loyalty all matter — the map is a living contest, not a checklist. The mechanics live in Protocol & contracts.
