Territory, capture & defense

Zones make progress spatial. Capture makes it yours. Decay and defense make it worth returning.

Local preview — the full system below runs as a single-player, on-device simulation in the current build.

What a zone represents

A zone is a hexagonal cell of the map — the game's atom of territory. It represents your relationship with a place: visited, held, neglected, defended. It does not represent land, property, or any real-world right, and holding a zone conveys nothing outside the game. (The hexagons come from the H3 geospatial grid — see Territory computation for the plain-language explanation.)

How routes become territory

When a session ends, the route is mapped to the zones it passed through. The summary shows the territory touched, and saving the session captures a zone into your persistent local portfolio, visible on the Today screen, your profile, the Territory Map, and a per-zone detail screen (the “zone command center”).

Zone lifecycle

  • Unexplored → Explored — a route passes through.
  • Captured — the zone joins your portfolio with a capture moment.
  • Stable → At risk — defense decays with neglect; alerts nudge you before a zone fades.
  • Fortified — moving through your zones refreshes defense, and an explicit Fortify action strengthens a zone further.

Districts

Zones group into named city districts (currently fictional groupings computed from your local zones). Districts carry their own mastery levels — long-term progression fed by capture, defense, activity, missions, and club presence — so neighborhoods you genuinely frequent become visibly yours.

Current implementation vs. the future model

What the territory system is today, and what it becomes
AspectToday (local preview)Future (planned)
CaptureOne zone per saved session, simulated on-deviceServer-verified capture of all qualifying zones on a route
ContestFictional “rival ghosts” create pressure; no real playersReal neighbors, clubs, and challenge mechanics
DefenseLocal decay + Fortify action + in-app alertsChallenge/defense model with real opponents, rate-limited and abuse-checked
PersistenceOn-device portfolio (AsyncStorage)Backend persistence behind verified routes

No mechanic in the current build spends, stakes, or consumes tokens — defense and fortification are free game actions. Any future challenge economics would be part of the gated Web3 layer and are documented separately as experimental.
