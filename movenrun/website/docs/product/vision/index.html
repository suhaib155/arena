Product vision

Make everyday movement so visibly worthwhile that people look forward to their next walk.

Mission

MovenRun helps people build healthier, more consistent movement habits by turning their own city into a game board. The mission is behavioral, not athletic: more people moving more often, at whatever pace fits their lives.

Target users

  • Habit builders — people who want to move more and need the loop to feel rewarding on day 40, not just day 1.
  • City explorers — walkers and cyclists who enjoy discovering streets, parks, and neighborhoods.
  • Friendly competitors — people motivated by clubs, streaks, and light territorial rivalry rather than race times.
  • Returning movers — people coming back from inactivity who need a game that celebrates showing up.

User needs we design for

  • Progress that is visible and spatial, not a buried statistic.
  • A reason to return to the same route — and a reason to try a new one.
  • Social motivation without leaderboard humiliation.
  • Trust: clear privacy boundaries and honest labels on what is real.

Product pillars

  1. Movement first. Every feature must serve the loop of moving, capturing, returning, and growing.
  2. Consistency over intensity. Territory strength comes from returning, not from pace. See Wellbeing & accessibility.
  3. A living city. Districts, clubs, rivals, events — the map should feel inhabited and slightly contested.
  4. Honest previews. Simulated systems say so, on the screen where they appear.
  5. Optional depth. Committed players can go deeper (clubs, mastery, and eventually optional ownership) without gating the basics.

What MovenRun is not

  • Not a medical product. No diagnosis, treatment, or health-outcome claims.
  • Not move-to-earn. There is no earning mechanic, token payout, or income framing — and no plan to lead with one.
  • Not a crypto product. Web3 is one optional layer inside the technology stack, not the identity of the product.
  • Not a race app. Pace matters less than presence; the game never punishes being slow.
  • Not real estate. Zones and Zone Deeds are digital game concepts, never real-world land.
