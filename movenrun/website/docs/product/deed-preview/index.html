Digital ownership

In most games, "your" territory is a row in someone else's database. In MovenRun, it's yours — an asset you hold onchain, provably, portably, permanently.

What you own

Every captured zone can become a Zone Deed: a unique onchain asset representing that piece of the map, held under your own keys. A deed isn't a badge the game grants and can revoke — it's property you control. You can hold it, strengthen it, defend it, and transfer it, and its history travels with it.

Why ownership changes the game

  • It persists. Your territory is still yours tomorrow, next season, next year.
  • It compounds. Active deeds earn standing and in-game yield, and combine into districts and city power.
  • It's portable. Ownership lives onchain, under your keys — not locked inside one app's account.
  • It's provable. Anyone can verify what you hold and how you earned it.

Ownership without the friction

Here's the part that makes it work for everyone: you get all of this without doing anything crypto. Your wallet is created for you, fees are covered, and claiming a deed feels like claiming a level in a game. If you're crypto-native, connect your own wallet and take full self-custody. If you're not, you'll own real assets and never have to think about how. See Onchain, made invisible and the technical detail in Zone Deeds.
