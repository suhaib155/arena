Progression

Progress in MovenRun is in-app, personal, and visible — and deliberately not a currency.

Implemented on-device in the current build.

XP, levels, and streaks

  • XP is earned by finishing sessions and completing objectives; it drives your level.
  • Streaks count consecutive active days. The daily rule is generous — one qualifying session keeps the streak alive — because the product optimizes for consistency, not volume.
  • Progress awards apply once per local day per goal, an anti-farming rule carried from the first builds.

Movement points and “Locked MOVE”

Session summaries show movement points. In the current app this counter carries the historical label Locked MOVE, and it is important to be precise about what it is:

Locked MOVE is a display preview, not a token. In the current build it is a value derived from your XP for preview purposes, labeled in-app as “preview · in-app progress, not a payout”. No balance is stored, nothing accrues, nothing is transferable, and nothing is redeemable. It exists to show what in-app progress could look like in a deeper design — not to promise one.

The distinction the product maintains everywhere: in-app progress (XP, streaks, movement points) is a game state on your device; transferable assets (tokens, NFTs) do not exist in the product today and would only ever arrive through the gated Web3 layer described in Token, economics & governance.

Collections and badges

Territory milestones — first capture, districts held, defense records — unlock collections and badges: read-only, on-device, and cosmetic. They make progress collectible without introducing rarity markets or value language.

Daily and weekly structure

  • Season objectives give each week a handful of concrete goals (capture, revisit, fortify, explore).
  • Weekly recap reflects the last 7 days of movement and territory change.
  • In-app alerts point at what needs attention next.

District mastery

Each district levels up from your long-term behavior in it: captures, defense, missions, events, rivals, and club presence. Mastery is the long arc of progression — the thing a year of morning walks builds. Local preview today, like the district system itself.
