Clubs, community & the city layer

Better habits are easier together. Clubs and the city layer make the map feel inhabited — today as clearly-labeled previews, later as real multiplayer.

Local preview — every system on this page runs on-device with fictional or locally-derived data. There is no real multiplayer, no live leaderboard, no real sponsors or events, and no networked play in the current build.

Clubs

Pick one of four local clubs (Base Builders, Riverside Runners, Campus Crew, Morning Miles). Your real local stats power your club's standing on a preview leaderboard, and a Club Territory dashboard connects your captured zones, districts, and rivals to your club — showing how club play will feel strategically, before it is networked.

Crew missions

A weekly missions board converts your territory, districts, rivals, and objectives into team-style goals. Missions are generated locally; the “crew” is illustrative until real memberships exist.

Rivals

Fictional “rival ghosts” apply light competitive pressure around your territory — contested zones, close calls, comebacks. They are simulated opponents, clearly labeled, with no connection to real users or anyone's real location.

Districts, city war, and city health

The city layer zooms out: districts aggregate zones, and a City War board stages a fictional season-long battle among the preview clubs, combining your districts, rivals, objectives, and weekly progress into one city-scale narrative.

Sponsor zones and event zones

Two preview systems show how the city layer could host real-world activations later: sponsor zones (how a brand-backed zone might look) and event zones (limited-time-style activity areas). Both are fictional demonstrations — no real sponsors, no ads, no payments, no live events — included so the future model is honest and visible rather than a surprise.
