The core loop

Move → Capture → Return → Grow. Four verbs that turn a walk into territory — with ownership as an optional layer for much later.

Move

A session begins with one tap. Walk, run, or cycle — the game treats all three as first-class. Foreground GPS records the route while live distance, time, and pace stay glanceable. There are no minimum speeds and no failure states for moving slowly.

Capture

Your route intersects hexagonal zones; finishing a session brings one of them into your territory with a small celebration. Capture is the moment the outing becomes a mark on the map. In the current build this is an on-device simulation (Local preview) — see Territory, capture & defense for exactly what that means.

Return

Territory softens with neglect. Moving through your zones refreshes them; a Fortify action strengthens them further; in-app alerts suggest what to revisit. This is the health mechanic wearing a game costume: the system that protects your territory is the same one that maintains your streak.

Grow

Consistency compounds into XP and levels, zone collections and badges, district mastery, season objectives, club standing, and weekly recaps. Growth is designed to be legible on the map, not just in a stats screen.

The historic “Own” layer

Earlier internal designs phrased the loop as Move → Capture → Defend → Own, with “Own” meaning on-chain Zone Deeds. Publicly, ownership is deliberately reframed as an optional deeper layer rather than a loop step: the loop must be complete without it, and today it exists only as an educational local preview plus experimental testnet contracts. No wallet is required for anything in the loop above.

The loop at three speeds

The same loop works for walking, running, and cycling

Walking20–40 min neighborhood loopSteady capture of home territory; streak-driven; districts grow slowly and durably
Running3–8 km routeWider reach per session; good for reclaiming at-risk zones and exploring adjacent districts
Cycling10–25 km rideBroad exploration; touches many zones lightly — returning still decides what you hold
