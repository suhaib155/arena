Wellbeing & accessibility

The game is tuned so that a 20-minute walk is a first-class move — because products that only celebrate athletes don't change habits.

Movement at every level

  • Walking, running, and cycling are equal citizens; no mode is “better”.
  • Territory strength comes from returning, not from pace or distance records.
  • Daily goals are achievable on a lunch break; streaks survive rest-day realities by design intent.
  • There are no public shame mechanics: previews compare you to fictional rivals, not to faster strangers.

Consistency over extreme performance

Everything compounding in the game — streaks, district mastery, collections, club contribution — is a function of showing up repeatedly. This is a deliberate inversion of race-oriented apps: MovenRun's strongest players are its most regular players.

Accessibility principles

  • Reduced motion respected. The website and docs honor prefers-reduced-motion end to end (static scenes, final values, no required animation); the app's design system uses gentle motion with no information conveyed by animation alone.
  • Readable by default. High-contrast light theme, large touch targets, semantic structure, and keyboard operability on the web surfaces.
  • No color-only meaning. Zone states pair color with labels and icons.
  • Interruptible. Sessions pause; nothing punishes stopping to rest.

Safety language

Live-session UI is glanceable so eyes stay on the road; alerts are in-app only; and no mechanic rewards risky behavior like sprinting across traffic to refresh a zone. Move where it is safe and legal to move.

No medical claims

MovenRun encourages regular movement — a generally healthy thing — but it is a game, not a health service. It does not diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any condition, does not give medical advice, and is not a substitute for professional guidance. If movement is medically risky for you, consult a professional before playing.
