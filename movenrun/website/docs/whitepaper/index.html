MovenRun Whitepaper

An onchain movement network on Base · MovenRun documentation

This whitepaper describes the MovenRun protocol, product, and economy as designed. MovenRun is a development-stage product; this document is informational and forward-looking, and the design may evolve. Nothing here is financial advice or a promise of returns. See Legal & disclaimers.

1. Introduction

MovenRun is an onchain network that turns real-world movement into ownable digital territory. Players move through their city and claim the ground they cover on a global hexagonal map; the territory they hold is recorded onchain as an asset they own, defend, trade, and build on. A native token, $MOVE, powers the economy, and the network is designed to be governed by its community. Critically, the entire experience is built to feel like a mainstream mobile game — the blockchain underneath is invisible to anyone who doesn't want to see it.

2. The problem

Human movement is enormous, constant, and almost entirely unmonetized in a way that benefits the mover. Fitness apps turn it into private statistics. Location platforms turn it into data they sell. The first wave of "move-to-earn" turned it into token emissions with no underlying utility, and those economies unravelled the moment sentiment shifted. Three failures recur: progress that isn't ownable, activity that can't be trusted, and onboarding that only crypto insiders can complete. MovenRun is designed around fixing all three.

3. Product thesis

Ownership is the most durable form of progress. A number in a fitness app resets; territory you own persists, appreciates in meaning as you build around it, and can be defended and traded. By making the map itself ownable and backing every claim with proof of real movement, MovenRun gives everyday activity a reason to matter that does not depend on token price. Three principles follow:

  • Own the map. Territory is an onchain asset, not a scoreboard.
  • Prove the movement. Every claim is backed by a verifiable proof of activity, so effort — not spoofing — wins.
  • Hide the plumbing. Onchain power with zero onchain friction, so the audience is everyone.

4. User experience

Onboarding is a single tap: an account and a secure wallet are created in the background, and the player is moving within seconds. A session records the route with live distance, time, and pace kept deliberately glanceable so attention stays on the road. Finishing produces a summary and a capture moment that claims territory. Around this core sit the systems that create depth — saved routes, streaks, seasons, districts, collections, and clubs.

5. The territory protocol

The world is modeled as a hexagonal grid using the open H3 system, so every point on Earth belongs to exactly one zone with a stable identifier. A route is converted into the ordered set of zones it passes through. Zones have a lifecycle — unexplored, captured, held, contested, fortified — and aggregate into districts and cities. Holding, defending, and upgrading territory are the central verbs of the game, and each maps to an onchain action. See Territory computation and Territory & ownership.

6. Proof of movement

An ownership economy is only as trustworthy as its inputs, so verifiable movement is foundational. Each session's signal is validated for physical plausibility — speed, accuracy, and continuity — reduced to a deterministic route proof, and attested by the network's oracle so that onchain contracts can act on movement without trusting the client. Duplicate and replayed routes are rejected. This proof layer is what lets territory be owned rather than merely displayed. See Movement verification.

7. Onchain architecture

MovenRun settles on Base, an Ethereum Layer 2 chosen for low fees, fast confirmation, and consumer-grade tooling. The protocol is a suite of contracts: the $MOVE token, Zone Deeds (the territory asset), a challenge system for contesting land, a season controller, a staking vault, and governance — all coordinated by the movement oracle. The application layer never holds custody of ownership; deeds and balances live onchain, under keys the user controls. See Architecture and Protocol & contracts.

8. Making onchain invisible

MovenRun's reach depends on removing every point of crypto friction. Wallets are embedded and provisioned automatically, so there are no seed phrases to write down. Gas is sponsored through account abstraction, so players never buy or think about fees. Complex actions are batched behind ordinary game buttons. Crypto-native users can connect their own wallet and take full self-custody at any time; everyone else simply plays. This is the bridge that lets an onchain network reach a mainstream audience. See Onchain, made invisible.

9. The $MOVE economy

$MOVE is the asset that powers MovenRun. Verified movement mints it within a capped, decaying schedule; utility across the game creates ongoing demand; and several core actions burn it, tightening supply as the network grows. Zone Deeds sit on top as the ownership layer, accruing standing and in-game yield to active holders. The economy is engineered utility-first: sinks and caps are designed before incentives so that value has reasons to persist. See the $MOVE economy.

10. Ownership: Zone Deeds

A Zone Deed is the onchain representation of a captured zone — one deed per map cell, held under the owner's keys. Deeds are transferable, carry provenance, accrue in-game yield and defensive strength while actively held, and can be challenged by other players through the contest system. They make territory a genuine asset class inside the game rather than a revocable database entry. See Zone Deeds.

11. Governance

MovenRun is designed to become community-owned. Governance is onchain and timelock-secured: token holders propose and vote on parameters, treasury allocation, and protocol upgrades, with a delay on execution that protects users. The network follows a progressive-decentralization path — beginning with active stewardship for safety and moving deliberately toward full community control as the system matures. See Governance & the DAO.

12. Roadmap

MovenRun advances in phases: a movement and capture beta that proves the loop; social and city systems that turn the map into a contest; onchain ownership and the live $MOVE economy on Base; and progressive decentralization of governance and treasury to the community. The roadmap details each phase and what it unlocks.

13. Risk factors

Building an onchain movement network carries real risks — movement verification versus spoofing, economic balance, smart-contract security, adoption density, and an evolving regulatory landscape. MovenRun addresses these with layered verification, utility-first economic design, security-by-design contracts, and a phased rollout. The full treatment is in Risk factors.
