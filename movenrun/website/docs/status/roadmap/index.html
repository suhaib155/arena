Roadmap & release gates

Health, trust, and a strong movement loop come first. Every later layer is gated by evidence — not by a launch calendar.

Phases carry exit criteria, not dates. Order and content may change as evidence arrives; that flexibility is deliberate and this page is the record of it.

Phase 1 — Health-first movement beta In progress

Reliable sessions, route summaries, goals, streaks, and territory progress with no wallet required. Most of this phase is live in the current on-device beta.

Exit criteria: the loop feels good over weeks, not sessions; routes map to zones reliably; early retention supports the thesis.

Phase 2 — Clubs and city challenges Planned

The local previews become real: accounts, verified activity, genuine club membership, local leaderboards, shared territory, and moderated city competition.

Exit criteria: multiplayer territory is stable, fair, and abuse-resistant at neighborhood scale; privacy review passes for any proximity feature.

Phase 3 — Verification & testnet ownership experiments Planned / partially built

The app connects to the existing verification infrastructure (route submission, validation, deduplication). Zone Deed experiments continue strictly on testnet, on the V2 contract design currently under review — not the defective V1.

Exit criteria: GPS verification is reliable against real-world abuse; the V2 design is merged, independently audited, and legally reviewed.

Phase 4 — City and partner expansion Conditional

City-by-city growth, real local events, disclosed partnerships, and only after everything above: consideration of a production ownership layer.

Exit criteria: the release gates below — all of them.

Release gates

These gates guard anything with real-world value attached. They are inherited from the project's standing internal guardrail: no liquid reward economy before reliable GPS verification, real density, and genuine demand.

  • Reliable GPS verification — spoofing and replay resistance proven in the wild, not in tests.
  • Abuse resistance — sybil, farming, and griefing economics understood and bounded.
  • Privacy readiness — retention/deletion controls shipped; policy reviewed by counsel.
  • User density — enough genuine players per city for territory to mean something.
  • Product retention — the game sustains habits without any economy.
  • Partner demand — real, disclosed sponsor interest (never fictional previews presented as traction).
  • Independent contract audit — internal tests, however extensive, do not count.
  • Legal review — jurisdiction-aware review of any asset or reward design.
  • Operational readiness — support, moderation, incident response.

No mainnet economy ships before these gates are met. No dates are promised for any of them.
