Roadmap

MovenRun is built in the right order: prove the movement loop, turn the map into a contest, bring ownership fully onchain, and hand the network to its community.

Phase 1 — Movement & capture

The foundation: fast onboarding, reliable movement sessions, verifiable proof of activity, and the capture loop that turns routes into territory. This phase proves the core experience feels great and that movement maps cleanly onto an ownable map.

Unlocks: sessions, route summaries, streaks, the territory map, and the first taste of owning the ground you cover.

Phase 2 — Clubs & city competition

The map becomes a contest. Clubs, crew missions, district control, city wars, and seasons turn individual holdings into team strategy and citywide rivalry, with the social depth that keeps a network alive.

Unlocks: clubs and leaderboards, shared territory, district mastery, and season-long city competition.

Phase 3 — Onchain ownership & the $MOVE economy

Ownership goes fully onchain on Base. Zone Deeds become live assets under players' keys, the $MOVE economy comes online with verified-movement minting and its full set of sinks and utility, and embedded wallets plus sponsored gas keep the whole thing effortless.

Unlocks: live Zone Deeds, the $MOVE economy, the challenge and staking systems, and true ownership of territory.

Phase 4 — Progressive decentralization

The network becomes its community's. Onchain governance takes on protocol parameters and the treasury, and control moves — deliberately and safely — from active stewardship toward full community ownership.

Unlocks: onchain proposals and voting, community treasury control, and the path to a self-governing network.

How we ship

MovenRun advances on evidence, not on a countdown. Each phase builds on a foundation that works: movement has to feel great before ownership goes live, and the economy and verification have to be sound before they carry real value. That sequencing is deliberate — it's how you build an onchain network people can trust. Timing and details evolve as the network grows; this page reflects the current plan.
