Frequently asked questions

Straight answers about what MovenRun is and how it works.

What is MovenRun?

An onchain movement network on Base. You move in the real world, capture the zones you cross on a global hex map, and own that territory as onchain assets called Zone Deeds. A native token, $MOVE, powers the economy, and the network is designed to be community-governed.

Do I really own my territory?

Yes. Zone Deeds are onchain assets held under keys you control — not a row in a database the operator can revoke. You can hold, defend, and transfer them, and take full self-custody whenever you want.

Do I need to know anything about crypto?

No. MovenRun creates a secure wallet for you automatically, covers gas fees, and hides all the blockchain machinery behind an ordinary game. You get real ownership without ever managing keys or buying a token first. See Onchain, made invisible.

Do I have to buy anything to start?

No. You start by moving. There's nothing to purchase to play, and onboarding is a single tap.

What chain is it on?

Base — an Ethereum Layer 2 chosen for low fees, fast confirmation, and the account-abstraction tooling that makes wallet-light onboarding possible.

What is $MOVE for?

$MOVE is the network's asset. It's minted by verified movement and used across the game — capturing and upgrading territory, entering challenges and seasons, staking, and governance. Several of those uses burn it, tightening supply as activity grows. See the $MOVE economy.

How does MovenRun know I actually moved?

Every session is validated for physical plausibility and reduced to a verifiable proof of movement that the network's oracle attests before anything settles onchain. Effort is rewarded; spoofing is rejected. See Movement verification.

What happens to my location data?

MovenRun is built to prove your activity without hoarding it — turning sessions into compact proofs rather than permanent trails, minimizing what's kept, and keeping your holdings as ownership records, not a movement profile. See Privacy & your data.

Who controls the network?

Governance is onchain and moving toward the community. Token holders propose and vote on parameters and the treasury, secured by a timelock, on a path of progressive decentralization. See Governance & the DAO.

Is everything live today?

MovenRun is a development-stage network being built in phases — movement and capture first, then city competition, then the full onchain economy, then progressive decentralization. The roadmap lays out the sequence, and this documentation describes the protocol as designed.
