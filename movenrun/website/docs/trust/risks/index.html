Risk factors

Building an onchain movement network is ambitious, and honesty about the risks is part of building it well. Here are the real ones — and how MovenRun's design addresses them.

Movement verification

Risk: location can be faked, and an ownership economy built on movement must resist spoofing. Approach: layered verification — plausibility checks, deterministic proofs, deduplication, and an oracle that attests activity onchain — designed to keep hardening as the network grows. Verification integrity is treated as foundational. See Movement verification.

Economic balance

Risk: movement economies have failed when incentives outran utility. Approach: MovenRun is designed utility-first — capped, decaying issuance; sinks and burns before incentives; ownership and demand woven through the game — so value has reasons to persist beyond emissions. Parameters are governed transparently onchain. See the $MOVE economy.

Smart-contract security

Risk: onchain systems are high-stakes and unforgiving of bugs. Approach: standards-based contracts, role-scoped permissions, timelocked governance, and defense in depth so no single failure is catastrophic. Security is designed in, not bolted on. See Security.

Adoption & density

Risk: a territory game is best where there are neighbors to contest. Approach: effortless, crypto-free onboarding to reach a mainstream audience, a game that's rewarding solo, and a phased city-by-city rollout that builds density before it's needed.

Regulatory landscape

Risk: rules for onchain assets vary across jurisdictions and continue to evolve. Approach: a design centered on utility and ownership rather than financial return, transparent governance, and a phased rollout that adapts as the landscape develops.

Location privacy

Risk: movement data is inherently sensitive. Approach: privacy-first design — proofs over raw trails, data minimization, no third-party ad tracking, and user control over assets and keys. See Privacy & your data.

MovenRun is a development-stage product; designs and timelines may change as the network matures. See Legal & disclaimers.
