Executive summary

MovenRun exists because staying active is easier when movement is visibly worth something — not financially, but personally: territory that grows, streaks that hold, a city that opens up, and a community that notices.

The opportunity

Most activity apps measure movement; few make people want to move again tomorrow. Habit research and a decade of fitness products point at the same gap: numbers alone don't sustain motivation, but visible progress, gentle social pressure, and play do. Meanwhile, "move-to-earn" products proved there is appetite for movement games, then collapsed under financial mechanics that punished ordinary users. MovenRun takes the motivating part — territory, progress, play — and deliberately leaves the speculative part out.

The product

MovenRun is a mobile movement game. You start a session, walk, run, or cycle, and your route lights up hexagonal zones around it. Zones you visit often become yours; zones you neglect fade. Districts, collections, clubs, crew missions, and city challenges give the loop long-term depth. The design language — Daylight Cartography — is bright, calm, and consumer-grade: a health product, not a trading dashboard.

Territory changes movement motivation because it converts an invisible habit into a visible, persistent, slightly contested map. A morning walk stops being a number and becomes defending Riverside.

Health-first, Web3-second

The public product leads with wellbeing: active time, consistency, exploration, and participation at every fitness level. Web3 appears only as an optional, clearly-labeled deeper layer: Zone Deeds as transparent digital territory records on Base — introduced only if and when they add real user value, and never required to play. MovenRun makes no medical claims and no financial promises.

Current maturity

  • Implemented — GPS movement sessions, route summaries, XP/streak progression, weekly recaps, an Android APK build pipeline, and a live marketing website.
  • Local preview — the entire territory game (capture, defense, clubs, districts, rivals, city wars, sponsor/event zones, deed showroom) runs on-device with deterministic or fictional data. No multiplayer servers yet.
  • Infrastructure — a backend route-verification pipeline (validation, deduplication, persistence, oracle signing, wallet-signature auth, rate limiting) is built and tested, but the consumer app does not submit routes to it yet.
  • Experimental testnet — a V1 contract suite is deployed on Base Sepolia. It is an experiment, not a product: a characterization audit of its known defects is Under review in an open pull request, alongside an isolated V2 redesign (source and tests only, not merged, not deployed).

Major risks and gates

The honest blockers are GPS spoofing and verification reliability, location privacy, city density (a territory game needs neighbors), contract security, and regulatory clarity around any future token. MovenRun's answer is sequencing: no liquid reward economy ships before reliable verification, real density, and genuine demand — a standing guardrail inherited from the project's canonical roadmap. The full list lives in Risks & limitations and Roadmap & release gates.
